Welcome to the life of Aaron Au! I am in the process of completing a Master’s of Chemical Engineering degree at the University of Toronto. Under the direction of Christopher Yip, I study and improve upon techniques for imaging micro-sized organisms to intercellular interactions. My role in the lab is to assemble microscopes given a set criteria, including budget. The Yip Lab is a great place to nurture interdisciplinary technical skills.

Before UofT, I graduated from the University of Waterloo’s Nanotechnology Engineering program. At Waterloo, I was a member of the varsity tennis team and the UW tennis club. Other than an amateur tennis player, I am also an amateur photographer. I usually take my camera with me whenever I leave the city. When travelling gets too expensive, I read books to escape the torments of the real world. I also read non-fiction, specifically scientific papers of any research field. By gathering knowledge from different areas, I hope to bring disruptive technologies to other fields.
